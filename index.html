Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should
know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws.
Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike.
This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a
new one?

1. Helmet is older than 5 years. ...
2. The interiors have started to come off. ...
3.The exterior is showing signs of wear & tear.

4. You can't get the stink off. ...
5. The helmet wobbles.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

1. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated,
it is normal after long periods of time on storage.
Inflate them close to the recommended level,
you can find that in the wall of the tire.

2. Make sure your drive train shifts gears correctly. Ride around and shift up and down
making sure you can access all your gear combinations. Cable stretching can also
make this not work correctly.

3. Your chain will probably need re-lubrication (or even replacement).
You will know this if it sounds off as you pedal your bike around.
